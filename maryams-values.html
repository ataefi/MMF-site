Maryam’s Values

The values that became her legacy.

Maryam’s life taught compassion, courage, sacrifice, hospitality, faith, humility, perseverance, education, service, and love — not as ideals, but as daily practice.

She did not perform goodness for attention.

She simply lived that way because love was written into the fabric of who she was.

The Foundation’s compass

What kind of human being was she?

The Foundation exists to carry forward the values Maryam lived so naturally: noticing others, giving without calculation, creating beauty, opening doors, protecting dignity, and loving without condition.

Compassion

She noticed suffering before it was spoken.

Maryam’s compassion was not passive. She did not wait for someone to ask for help, explain their pain, or prove they deserved care. She noticed. She moved toward people. She gave quietly.

Whether it was a stranger at Costco, a lonely friend, a frightened child, a nurse who needed tenderness, or someone silently carrying hardship, Maryam responded with dignity and warmth.

Education

She believed learning could transform a life.

From a young age, Maryam loved books, learning, and self-education. Before formal classrooms shaped her, she shaped herself through reading, poetry, art, craft, and curiosity.

That love of education followed her through Iran, Pakistan, and America — into college, computer science, teaching, and the countless ways she helped others believe they could become more.

Faith

She lived as though every soul belonged to God.

Maryam’s faith was not something separate from her daily life. It was present in the way she prayed, served, forgave, welcomed, sacrificed, and loved.

She prayed constantly for others — for healing, comfort, protection, success, and peace. Her prayers were acts of service, offered with purity and devotion.

Courage

She chose her children, time and time again.

Maryam endured revolution, religious persecution, immigration, hardship, illness, and loss. But suffering did not make her bitter. It made her more determined to protect, provide, and love.

When circumstances demanded courage, she did not retreat. She carried her family forward with strength, faith, and a calmness that made others feel safe even when the road ahead was uncertain.

Sacrifice

She gave even when it cost her everything.

Maryam gave of herself tirelessly. She worked, studied, raised children, cared for family, created beauty, cooked, prayed, and carried burdens that many never fully saw.

Her sacrifice was not dramatic or self-pitying. It was steady. It was woven into ordinary days. She gave because love, to her, was action.

Hospitality

Her table made people feel expected.

Maryam’s home was a refuge. She cooked with abundance, welcomed people with tenderness, and made guests feel not only invited, but cherished.

For two people or two hundred, she loved with the same fullness. Her hospitality was not presentation. It was protection, nourishment, celebration, and care.

Service

She served without expecting anything in return.

Maryam did not need recognition. She did not seek praise. She simply saw what needed to be done and did it with love.

She served family, friends, strangers, children, elders, students, and anyone in need. The Foundation’s work is a continuation of that service.

Love

She loved completely.

Maryam loved in a way that did not depend on whether someone deserved it. Her love was steady, warm, forgiving, and full.

To be loved by her was to feel safe. To be near her was to feel seen. And for those whose lives she touched, that love became a compass.

To carry Maryam’s values forward is to love more deeply, give more freely, notice one another more gently, and remember that even the smallest act of kindness can illuminate someone’s entire world.

Carry the values forward

This is how her light becomes action.

Through scholarships, mentorship, education, creative programs, community support, and compassionate service, the Foundation seeks to turn Maryam’s values into living work.
