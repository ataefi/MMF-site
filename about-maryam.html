About Maryam

Why did one woman change so many lives?

Maryam Mottaghi was a woman of deep faith, extraordinary tenderness, quiet strength, and unconditional love. Her life was not measured by recognition, but by the people she protected, encouraged, fed, taught, prayed for, and carried.

“Some people are loved in this world.

Very few become a force through which love continuously flows outward to everyone around them.”

Her presence

A life that gave more than it ever asked to receive.

Maryam loved not as an idea, but as a daily practice. She noticed people. She welcomed people. She prayed for people. She created beauty around her and safety within her home.

To be loved by her was to feel seen, protected, and remembered. The Foundation exists so that her way of loving continues reaching people she never had the chance to meet.

What she embodied

Maryam’s values were not words. They were actions.

Compassion

She responded to suffering with tenderness, not distance.

Courage

She protected others even when doing so required sacrifice.

Hospitality

She made every person feel expected, welcome, and worthy of care.

Faith

She lived as though every human soul belonged to God.

Education

She believed learning could open doors, restore dignity, and transform lives.

Love

She loved fully, quietly, and without condition.

Education and service

She believed in becoming, learning, and helping others rise.

Maryam’s legacy lives naturally through education, mentorship, creativity, practical support, and service. These were not separate from who she was. They were part of how she loved.

Every program of the Foundation is rooted in the same spirit: dignity, opportunity, compassion, and light.
